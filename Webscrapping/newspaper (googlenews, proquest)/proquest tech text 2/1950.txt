TWO South Australian experts in artificial intelligence and cyber security will help drive investment in space technology research and development.
Adelaide University’s Associate Professor Tat-Jun Chin and UniSA’s Professor Jill Slay are among the first appointments to a $20m nine-member unit tasked with developing the next generation of technologies to stimulate Australia’s space industry. They will form an advisory group under the Lot Fourteen-based SmartSat Co-operative Research Centre to refine programs and boost translation of research into industry application.
