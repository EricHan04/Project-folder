ADELAIDE University will receive $3.7 million in federal funding for a data-focused centre to aid the mining industry, it will be announced today.
Education Minister Dan Tehan said the centre, which will train budding scientists and engineers in advanced sensors and data analytics, would help the industry “better use data to make evidence-based decisions that lead to more efficient operations”.
It is aimed at raising certainty on mineral quality and maximising resource recovery.Adelaide University deputy vice-chancellor Mike Brooks said it would “help to shape the very future of mining operations in Australia”.
