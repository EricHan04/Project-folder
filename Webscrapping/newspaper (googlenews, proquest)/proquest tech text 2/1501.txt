A CUTTING-edge artificial intelligence tool being piloted in Victorian schools flags struggling readers and within minutes comes up with solutions to improve their grades.
Eleven government and private schools are trialling Lexplore literacy analytics, with principals already lauding the results. Under the Swedish program, a child reads a passage both aloud and silently. An eye tracker is calibrated to pick up any issues, including “micropauses” indicating which letter-sound combinations the student is struggling to identify. The system then generates a report suggesting the most suitable interventions.
