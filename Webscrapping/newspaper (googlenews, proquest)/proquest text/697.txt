FOUR Adelaide technology companies have been awarded $3.1 million in federal funding to commercialise their inventions.
Signostics received $1.6 million to develop a hand-held diagnostic device for doctors.
Scantech gained a Commercial Readygrant of $1.2 million for its online analysis instrument for mining companies.
Reproductive Health Science, was awarded $144,003 to develop its gene tests.
The Touche Adaptive Systems, a venture with Kojo, also received a grant to develop its digital signage.
