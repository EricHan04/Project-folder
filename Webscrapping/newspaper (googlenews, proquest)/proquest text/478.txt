AUSTRALIA may have months to wait before it learns the full impact of a Test match which it took too lightly for its own good.
While Steve Waugh's team took little time to rediscover its destructive groove, the lack of any cricket in the weeks leading up to the two Test series against Zimbabwe has returned to haunt them.
Injuries to Jason Gillespie and Stuart MacGill have cast a storm cloud over Australia's attack that is as bleak as the one that drenched the WACA yesterday afternoon.
In recent years, Australia's fast bowlers have been afforded a lighter training workload to compensate for a logjammed international program. But even the best need to ramp-up their training tempo before competing at the highest level.
Coach John Buchanan revealed he would have liked at least one trial game to prepare.
