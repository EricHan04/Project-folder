ADELAIDE-based medical devices company Signostics has won a Good Design Award from Chicago's Museum of Architecture and Design for its Signos personal ultrasound device.
The device, the smallest portable ultrasound available, was designed by Sydney design consultancy Design+Industry.
Signostics chief operating officer Stewart Bartlett said the product was "a true visual stethoscope".
The Signos device was developed with assistance from Ausindustry's Commercial Ready and Biotechnology Innovation Fund programs.
