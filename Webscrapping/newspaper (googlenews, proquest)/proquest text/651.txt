ADELAIDE-BASED company Signostics has won two prizes at the 2010 Australian International Design Awards for its handheld ultrasound device.
The Signos won a design award in the medical and scientific category and the Powerhouse Museum Design Award, which recognises a product which has the potential to become important in the lives of Australians.
"The worldwide personal ultrasound market is estimated to be worth $US3 billion and we believe our Signos can capture a significant slice of that," said chief operating officer Stewart Bartlett.
