NSW and South Australia’s electricity grids will be connected for the first time after the Berejiklian government approved stage one of a new long-awaited $1.8bn transmission line that will create cheaper, more reliable power, saving consumers almost $200m year.
Construction on the two stage 900km transmission link — known as Energy Connect — will finally begin, feeding more renewable energy into the states’ power grids. The first stage will connect NSW, from the Buronga substation in the state’s south-west, to South Australia.
