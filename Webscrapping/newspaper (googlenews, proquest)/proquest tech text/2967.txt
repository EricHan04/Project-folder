UNIVERSITY open days will be held this weekend for the first time since the start of the pandemic.
Flinders University will host prospective students at its Bedford Park campus on Saturday, while the University of Adelaide and University of South Australia will open their city facilities on Sunday.
Events at Flinders include a mock autopsy, virtual reality demonstrations, blood splatter analysis, an archaeological dig, dissection of a shark and preparation of a solar car for racing.
UniSA will open facilities such as its simulated hospital environments, laboratories, high-performance sport centre, financial trading centre, mock court and art studios. The University of Adelaide also has a range of activities, including its classical archaeology museum and acoustic sound chambers.
