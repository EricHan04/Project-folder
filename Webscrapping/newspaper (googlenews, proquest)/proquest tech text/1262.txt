BILLIONAIRE SpaceX founder Elon Musk has found himself in hot water with China after his satellites strayed a little too close to the superpower’s space station orbiting Earth.
State media took the world’s richest man to task over two separate incidents in July and October, where SpaceX satellites drifted dangerously close to China’s space station. In a note filed with the UN’s space agency in December, critics claimed the SpaceX vessel had potential to put “life or health of astronauts” on board in danger. “For safety reasons, the China Space Station implemented preventive collision avoidance control,” Chinese representatives said.
