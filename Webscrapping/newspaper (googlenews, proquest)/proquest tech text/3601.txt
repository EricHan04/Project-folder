From page 1 Funding that was since been secured by the council have helped people directly affected by the blaze, along with projects to help revitalise the area.
"Council secured $400,000 of funding for individual property owners along with innovative well-being, community, and tourism initiatives," council stated.
"Just 12 months after the fire, using tourism bushfire recovery funds, ashes have been turned into art with the delivery of SA's first augmented reality public artwork."
The council stated the tourism project, in relation to its newly implemented artwork along the main street, is not only a lasting and poignant recognition of the forgotten fire, it has created incredible economic reward.
The award is described as one of the highest accolades for the local government sector in South Australia.
